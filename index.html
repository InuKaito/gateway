Tokyo, Japan

Overview

Tokyo was once a called Edo where Tokugawa Ieyasu established the Tokugawa Shogunate here in 1603. Since then Edo became the center of politics and culture in Japan. Until the Meiji Restoration in 1868, when the Tokugawa Shogunate was defeated and the imperial rule was restored. The emperor then moved to Edo and renamed Tokyo and it then became the capital of Japan.
